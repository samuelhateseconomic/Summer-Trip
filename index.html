Day-by-Day Expedition Itinerary

Friday, July 3rd 258 mi · Night 1 at Sequoia

Day 1 — Ascent to the Giant Forest

Departing Santa Ana at midnight to bypass 100% of Friday morning gridlock, the loop ascends the western Sierra foothills to arrive at Sequoia National Park at first light — earning a misty, uncrowded dawn inside the world's largest trees.

Transit Corridor: I-5 N → CA-99 N through Bakersfield at dawn → CA-198 E into the Sierra foothills.
Night 1 Basecamp: Hotel lodging inside Sequoia National Park (~6,200 ft elevation).
00:00 AM

Midnight Launch from Santa Ana

The loop begins. Clear open freeway lanes as you cruise north through the night. Bakersfield appears as first light breaks on the horizon.

05:00 AM

Sequoia National Park Arrival

Enter the Giant Forest as dawn mist still clings to the switchbacks. You have the entire park practically to yourselves.

Morning Focus

Moro Rock & The Giant Sequoias

Moro Rock Trail: Tackle the 350-step granite staircase in cool morning air. The summit rewards you with an unobstructed, misty panorama of the Great Western Divide — zero crowds.

General Sherman Tree: Walk into the grove to stand beneath the single largest living tree on Earth by volume (52,500 cubic feet of wood).

Tunnel Log: Drive your vehicle through the hollowed-out fallen giant tree for the classic group photo.

Afternoon

Settle In & Explore

Check into the hotel and unwind. Spend the afternoon relaxing under towering ancient boughs. Prepare tomorrow's early Kings Canyon drive.

Night 1

Sleep Under the Redwood Stars

Settle in for Night 1 among the sequoias. Enjoy the deep silence of the ancient forest and a sky full of stars at 6,200 ft elevation.

Scenery & Highlights

Saturday, July 4th ~270 mi · Night 2 at Lake Tahoe

Day 2 — Canyon Depths to Cobalt Shores

Rise early in the Sequoia forest, drive the historic Generals Highway north to Kings Canyon for a morning of canyon exploration, then cross into the alpine basin of Lake Tahoe — with an optional 4th of July detour to witness the roaring Burney Falls.

Transit Corridor: Generals Hwy (Sequoia → Kings Canyon), then CA-41/US-50 or the direct route north to South Lake Tahoe.
Night 2 Basecamp: Resort cabin lodging in South Lake Tahoe for Nights 2 & 3.
07:00 AM

Dawn Wake-Up in the Giant Forest

Watch morning sunbeams cut through the sequoia grove before packing up and heading north.

08:30 AM

Kings Canyon National Park

Drive the historic Generals Highway north (30 miles, ~1.2 hours) to Kings Canyon.

General Grant Tree: Walk the short paved loop in Grant Grove to see the "Nation's Christmas Tree."

Zumwalt Meadow: Stroll the boardwalk loop along the glacier-carved canyon floor to watch the Kings River reflect raw granite cliffs.

11:30 AM

Optional: Burney Falls Detour

Saturday Loop

Burney Falls — 129-ft Basalt Cascade

Drive north to McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park to witness 100 million gallons of spring-fed water per day erupting from a moss-covered basalt cliff. Day-use vehicle fee: $11.

Trade-off: Adds ~4 hours of driving (~340 mi round trip) vs. the direct Tahoe route. However, it clears Sunday completely for a relaxed Tahoe day.

Route map shows: Direct KC → Tahoe

05:30 PM

South Lake Tahoe Arrival

Check into your lakeside cabin. Grab a celebratory July 4th dinner in town and watch your first high-altitude sunset over the cobalt alpine water.

Scenery & Highlights

Sunday, July 5th 0 mi driving · Full Tahoe Day · Night 3

Day 3 — The Emerald Cascades & Sunset Cliffs

With long drives behind you, Sunday is dedicated entirely to Lake Tahoe. A leisurely lakeside morning, a spectacular hike to Eagle Falls and Emerald Bay, and a golden-hour scramble to the top of Cave Rock for a 180-degree panoramic sunset.

Focus: Eagle Falls Trail, Emerald Bay & Fannette Island overlook, Cave Rock East Shore scramble.
Night 3 Basecamp: Final night in South Lake Tahoe. Celebrate the loop's high point.
08:00 AM

Leisurely Lakeside Morning

Enjoy a long breakfast by the shore. Watch the morning light turn the water from deep black to brilliant teal. No rush — the whole day belongs to the lake.

10:30 AM

Eagle Falls & Emerald Bay Optional · ~2h extra drive

Optional Hike

Eagle Falls Trail

Emerald Bay State Park is located on the west shore of Lake Tahoe (~15 miles from South Lake Tahoe) — around a 30-minute drive each way on winding scenic roads. Budget ~2 extra hours total for the round trip and the hike itself.

The reward: a spectacular overlook of Tahoe's most brilliant teal inlet and Fannette Island sitting alone in the bay. The falls thunder alongside you on the climb up.

Note: If your group prefers a more relaxed Sunday, skip the drive and enjoy kayaking or paddleboarding directly at the South Shore instead.
02:00 PM

Lakeside Afternoon Recreation

Return to South Lake Tahoe for a relaxed afternoon. Options include kayak and paddleboard rentals, swimming at Pope Beach, or simply walking the waterfront boardwalk.

05:30 PM

Cave Rock Sunset Scramble

Drive to the East Shore (Nevada side) and scramble to the top of Cave Rock. The granite summit provides an unforgettable, unobstructed 180-degree panorama as the sun sinks below the Sierra crest, painting the entire lake in copper and gold.

08:00 PM

Final Night Celebration

Return to South Lake Tahoe for a proper celebration dinner — your last night at altitude. Share the day's photos and toast the Sierra Ascent Loop.

Scenery & Highlights

Monday, July 6th ~475 mi · US-395 Return · Loop Complete

Day 4 — The US-395 Eastern Descent

The final leg sweeps south along the spectacular Eastern Sierra escarpment on US-395 — one of America's most dramatic highway corridors. A lunch stopover in Mammoth Lakes breaks the drive before descending through the Alabama Hills and back to Orange County.

Transit Corridor: US-395 South — Eastern Sierra escarpment past Mono Lake, Mammoth Lakes, Mt. Whitney, and the Alabama Hills.
Loop Closure: Completing the full 723-mile Sierra Nevada loop back to Santa Ana / Orange County.
09:00 AM

Lakeside Check-Out

A slow, peaceful final breakfast in South Lake Tahoe. Walk along the shoreline one last time before loading the vehicle and heading south on US-395.

12:15 PM

Mammoth Lakes Stopover (165 miles)

Arrive in the mountain village for a scenic lunch break. Two optional diversions:

Minaret Vista: Drive up to the overlook for a jaw-dropping view of the volcanic Ritter Range and Banner Peak.

Convict Lake: Stretch your legs with a short stroll along the crystalline, swirled granite banks of this hidden alpine gem.

02:30 PM

Continue the Southern Descent

Head south on US-395, passing the Alabama Hills (classic Western film locations), Lone Pine, and the Owens Valley floor — towering Mt. Whitney and the Sierra crest to your right the entire way.

07:30 PM

Loop Complete — Arrive Santa Ana

Return to Orange County with the Sierra Ascent Loop fully closed: 723 miles, 4 days, 3 nights, and a lifetime of mountain memories.

The Return Corridor

US-395 South — one of America's most spectacular highways. The Eastern Sierra crest accompanies you the entire 475 miles home.

🏔️ SIERRA ASCENT ROAD TRIP PLAN 🏔️
Departing Santa Ana: Friday morning, July 4th @ 00:00 AM (midnight Thursday night!)
Trip Length: 4 Days & 3 Nights of high-altitude exploration!

📍 OUR STOPS:
1. Santa Ana (00:00 AM departure basecamp)
2. Sequoia National Park (staying Night 1 in a hotel!)
3. Kings Canyon National Park (Saturday morning Grant Grove hike)
4. Lake Tahoe & Emerald Bay (staying Nights 2 & 3 at the lake!)
5. Mammoth Lakes (Monday afternoon return stopover)

🥾 KEY HIKES & SIGHTS:
• Climb Moro Rock granite staircase for a misty sunrise panorama
• Stand under the General Sherman & General Grant trees
• Hike to Eagle Falls & explore stunning Emerald Bay
• Scramble to Cave Rock for 180-degree Tahoe sunset views
• Optional: road trip to the majestic 129-ft Burney Falls ($11 fee)
• Scenic stop at Convict Lake in Mammoth on the drive back

🎒 PREPARATION:
• Pack clothes for 4 days & 3 nights (include HEAVY layers for cold nights at 6,200ft!)
• Bring a cooler with food & solid ice blocks for all 4 days
• Reusable water flask (min 6L per person — altitude dehydration is real!)
• High-SPF sunscreen and bug spray for the hikes
• Download offline maps before we roll out (zero cell service in the parks!)
• Budget ~$500 for food, gas, fees & activities
