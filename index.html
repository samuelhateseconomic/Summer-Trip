Day-by-Day Expedition Itinerary

Friday, July 4th ~500 mi · Night 1 at Lake Tahoe

Day 1 — Drive North & Settle into Tahoe

The loop begins at midnight Thursday — a smart departure that clears 100% of Southern California Fourth of July gridlock. A morning detour to the roaring 129-ft Burney Falls breaks the journey before arriving lakeside at Tahoe by late afternoon.

Transit Corridor: I-5 N through the Central Valley → CA-299 E to Burney Falls → Lassen Corridor south toward Lake Tahoe (~2.5 hrs from Burney).
Night 1 Basecamp: Hotel lodging at Lake Tahoe — check in, relax, dinner lakeside.
12:00 AM

Midnight Launch from Santa Ana

The loop begins Thursday night at midnight. Open freeway lanes all the way up I-5 North through the Central Valley — zero holiday traffic at this hour.

10:00 AM

Burney Falls — 129-ft Basalt Cascade

Arrive at McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park. Walk the flat paved loop trail around the stunning 129-foot spring-fed falls — 100 million gallons of water per day erupting from moss-covered basalt.

⚠️ Holiday Weekend: A pre-booked 2026 day-use parking reservation is required — book ahead as this is a popular July 4th destination.
1:30 PM

Depart South via Lassen Corridor

Head south through the dramatic Lassen volcanic corridor toward Lake Tahoe — approximately 2.5 hours of scenic mountain driving.

4:00 PM

Arrive Lake Tahoe — Check In & Relax

Check into your Tahoe hotel and unwind. Tonight is all about soaking in the lakeside atmosphere — a well-earned dinner with the cobalt water shimmering outside your window.

Scenery & Highlights

Saturday, July 5th ~local driving · Night 2 at Lake Tahoe

Day 2 — Tahoe Highlights

A full day dedicated to Lake Tahoe's greatest hits — turquoise granite-boulder beaches, a hidden Caribbean-colored cove, the legendary Eagle Falls trail at Emerald Bay, and a dramatic sunset scramble above the lake. Go early to beat the holiday weekend crowds.

Focus: Sand Harbor, Secret Cove, Emerald Bay + Eagle Falls, Cave Rock sunset scramble.
Night 2 Basecamp: Second night at Lake Tahoe hotel — celebrate the day's highlights over dinner.
8:00 AM

Sand Harbor State Park

Arrive early to grab a prime beach spot among the iconic turquoise water and massive granite boulders. One of the most photogenic lakeshores in North America — stunning in the morning calm before the holiday crowds arrive.

11:30 AM

Secret Cove Beach

An easy ~1-mile round-trip trail leads down to a hidden, Caribbean-colored cove with dramatically clear water. A local treasure well worth the short hike.

2:00 PM

Emerald Bay Overlook & Eagle Falls Trail

Drive to Emerald Bay State Park for a breathtaking overlook of Tahoe's most brilliant teal inlet and Fannette Island. Then tackle the Eagle Falls trail as the waterfall thunders alongside you on the climb.

⚠️ Holiday Buffer: Build in an extra 2 hours of buffer here for holiday weekend parking and traffic congestion along the west shore.
7:30 PM

Cave Rock Sunset Scramble

Drive to the East Shore (Nevada side) for a short, steep scramble to the top of Cave Rock. The granite summit provides an unforgettable 180° panorama as the sun sinks below the Sierra crest, painting the entire lake in copper and gold.

Scenery & Highlights

Sunday, July 6th ~280 mi · Night 3 at Sequoia

Day 3 — Onward to Sequoia

Leave Tahoe Sunday morning and drive south through the dramatic Sierra passes to Sequoia National Park. Cool off with a swim in the Kaweah River, conquer Moro Rock's staircase, and stand beneath the world's largest tree. Important: temperatures drop fast after sunset under the canopy — dry off and layer up.

Transit Corridor: South through the Sierra passes from Lake Tahoe to Sequoia National Park (~5 hrs, ~280 mi).
Night 3 Basecamp: Hotel in the Sequoia area. Temperatures drop fast after sunset — switch into warm layers after swimming.
8:30 AM

Depart Lake Tahoe

Check out and begin the southbound drive through the Sierra Nevada passes. Approximately 5 hours of mountain scenery to Sequoia.

2:00 PM

Kaweah River Swim & Giant Sequoias

Arrive at Sequoia and cool off with a refreshing swim in the Kaweah River before tackling the park's iconic landmarks.

Moro Rock: Climb the 350-step granite staircase for an unobstructed panorama of the Great Western Divide — the ultimate Sierra viewpoint.

General Sherman Tree: Stand beneath the single largest living tree on Earth by volume (52,500 cubic feet of wood).

🌡️ Cold After Dark: Temperatures drop fast once the sun sets under the Sequoia canopy. Dry off completely after swimming and switch into warm layers — hoodie, fleece, and pants — to avoid hypothermia risk.
Evening

Check Into Sequoia-Area Hotel

Settle in for Night 3 among the ancient giants. Enjoy a quiet evening under the redwood canopy and rest up for tomorrow's Kings Canyon morning.

Scenery & Highlights

Monday, July 7th ~290 mi · Return to Santa Ana

Day 4 — Kings Canyon & Drive Home

The final morning belongs to Kings Canyon — the General Grant Tree and the spectacular Zumwalt Meadow boardwalk along the glacier-carved canyon floor. Then it's a smooth 5-hour drive home via CA-99/I-5, closing the loop back in Santa Ana by late afternoon.

Transit Corridor: Kings Canyon (~30 mi from Sequoia) → CA-99 South / I-5 South back to Santa Ana (~5 hrs, ~290 mi).
Loop Closure: Completing the full Sierra Nevada loop back to Santa Ana by 5:30 PM.
8:00 AM

Kings Canyon — Grant Grove & Zumwalt Meadow

General Grant Tree: Walk the short paved loop in Grant Grove to see the "Nation's Christmas Tree" — the second largest living tree on Earth and the only living thing designated a national shrine.

Zumwalt Meadow Boardwalk: Stroll the boardwalk loop along the glacier-carved canyon floor and watch the Kings River reflect raw 8,000-ft granite walls overhead.

12:00 PM

Lunch & Depart for Home

Grab a final lunch in the park or a foothill town, then head home via CA-99 South / I-5 South. The drive takes approximately 5 hours through the Central Valley.

5:30 PM

Loop Complete — Arrive Santa Ana

Return to Orange County with the Sierra Ascent Loop fully closed: ~700 miles, 4 days, 3 nights, and memories of waterfalls, cobalt lakes, and ancient giants.

🏔️ SIERRA ASCENT ROAD TRIP PLAN 🏔️
Departing Santa Ana: Friday July 4th @ 12:00 AM (midnight Thursday night!)
