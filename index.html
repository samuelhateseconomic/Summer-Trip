Day-by-Day Expedition Itinerary

Friday, July 4th ~430 mi · Night 1 at Reno Airbnb

Day 1 — West Shore & The Drive into Reno

Midnight departure from Santa Ana straight up I-5 North — no Burney Falls detour, so the drive is a direct 7–7.5 hours. Catch Emerald Bay at first light, hike Eagle Falls, grab lunch in South Lake Tahoe, then cross the Sierra into Reno for the first of two nights at the Airbnb.

Transit Corridor: I-5 N → CA-99 N → US-50 West Shore (Emerald Bay) → US-50 E through Spooner Summit into Carson City → US-395 N into Reno.
Night 1 Basecamp: Reno Airbnb — unpack, grab groceries, cook dinner and recover. First real chance to sit down since midnight.
12:00 AM

Midnight Launch from Santa Ana

Open freeway lanes all the way up I-5 North through the Central Valley — zero holiday traffic at this hour. No detour north this time, so the drive is direct to the West Shore.

7:00 AM

Emerald Bay Overlook

One of the most photographed views in the Sierra. Lake Tahoe is the largest alpine lake in North America and the second-deepest lake in the U.S. — you’ll see why the water looks so impossibly clear here.

Bonus (time permitting): Vikingsholm, a 1929 Scandinavian-style mansion at the bottom of the bay, is reachable via a steep ~1-mile trail down and back up.

8:00 AM

Eagle Falls Hike

The trailhead shares the same parking area as the Emerald Bay overlook — no driving between the two. A moderate ~2-mile round trip gets you to the upper falls. Continue on if you want to push to Eagle Lake.

11:30 AM

Drive to South Lake Tahoe

About 20–25 minutes south on Hwy 89 into South Lake Tahoe.

12:00 PM

Lunch — The Chateau at the Village

On Lake Tahoe Blvd near Heavenly Village/Stateline. Dumpling 85 and McP’s Irish Pub are both on site if you want to wander before deciding.

1:00 PM

Drive to Reno

About 1 hour via US-50 East over Spooner Summit and through Carson City, then north on US-395 into Reno. The most direct route — no need to retrace the morning’s path.

2:00 PM

Arrive Reno — Settle into the Airbnb

Unpack, grab any groceries you still need, and rest up before cooking. First real chance to sit down since the midnight drive — take it easy tonight.

Scenery & Highlights

Saturday, July 5th ~local driving · Night 2 at Reno Airbnb

Day 2 — East Shore, Linear from Reno

A full day on Lake Tahoe’s stunning Nevada East Shore — linear from the Reno Airbnb base. Sand Harbor at 8 AM for the turquoise coves, then Secret Cove, the rugged Skunk Harbor hike, and the dramatic Cave Rock sunset scramble before driving back to the same Airbnb for Night 2.

Focus: Sand Harbor, Secret Cove, Skunk Harbor, Cave Rock sunset scramble. All linear from Reno via Mt. Rose Hwy (NV-431).
Night 2 Basecamp: Same Reno Airbnb — drive straight back via US-50 East over Spooner Summit, no check-in needed.
7:00 AM

Depart Reno

Take Mt. Rose Highway (NV-431) toward Incline Village — about 40–45 minutes down to the lakefront.

8:00 AM

Sand Harbor State Park

Famous for its turquoise coves and granite boulders. Lake Tahoe’s water is about 99.994% pure — exactly why it looks this clear. Arrive early for the best spot.

⚠️ Reservation Required: Nevada State Parks requires a day-use reservation (reservenevada.com) for entry before 10:30 AM during peak season — book ahead for this July 4th-weekend Saturday.
11:00 AM

Secret Cove

About 20 minutes further south on Hwy 28. A short trail down to a hidden, Caribbean-colored cove with dramatically clear water.

1:00 PM

Skunk Harbor

Roughly 1.5 miles each way down an old logging road — look for remnants of an 1870s railroad grade along the way. The cove at the bottom has a stone picnic house built in the 1920s by a San Francisco family.

🦺 Pack It Out: No restrooms or trash service at Skunk Harbor. Bring a packed picnic and pack out everything you bring in.
3:30 PM

Drive Toward Cave Rock

Continue south on Hwy 28/50 toward the south end of the East Shore — about 20–25 minutes.

7:30 PM

Cave Rock Sunset Scramble

A short, steep climb for a 180° sunset view over the Sierra — the final stop of the day as the sun sinks below the crest, painting the entire lake copper and gold.

8:00 PM

Drive Back to Reno

About 1 hour via US-50 East over Spooner Summit and Carson City. Same Airbnb as Friday night — head straight back, no check-in needed.

Scenery & Highlights

Sunday, July 6th ~280 mi · Night 3 at Sequoia

Day 3 — Onward to Sequoia

Leave Tahoe Sunday morning and drive south through the dramatic Sierra passes to Sequoia National Park. Cool off with a swim in the Kaweah River, conquer Moro Rock's staircase, and stand beneath the world's largest tree. Important: temperatures drop fast after sunset under the canopy — dry off and layer up.

Transit Corridor: South through the Sierra passes from Lake Tahoe to Sequoia National Park (~5 hrs, ~280 mi).
Night 3 Basecamp: Hotel in the Sequoia area. Temperatures drop fast after sunset — switch into warm layers after swimming.
8:30 AM

Depart Reno

Check out of the Airbnb and begin the southbound drive through the Sierra Nevada. Approximately 5 hours to Sequoia via US-395 S and CA-168 W.

2:00 PM

Kaweah River Swim & Giant Sequoias

Arrive at Sequoia and cool off with a refreshing swim in the Kaweah River before tackling the park's iconic landmarks.

Moro Rock: Climb the 350-step granite staircase for an unobstructed panorama of the Great Western Divide — the ultimate Sierra viewpoint.

General Sherman Tree: Stand beneath the single largest living tree on Earth by volume (52,500 cubic feet of wood).

🌡️ Cold After Dark: Temperatures drop fast once the sun sets under the Sequoia canopy. Dry off completely after swimming and switch into warm layers — hoodie, fleece, and pants — to avoid hypothermia risk.
Evening

Check Into Sequoia-Area Hotel

Settle in for Night 3 among the ancient giants. Enjoy a quiet evening under the redwood canopy and rest up for tomorrow's Kings Canyon morning.

Scenery & Highlights

Monday, July 7th ~290 mi · Return to Santa Ana

Day 4 — Kings Canyon & Drive Home

The final morning belongs to Kings Canyon — the General Grant Tree and the spectacular Zumwalt Meadow boardwalk along the glacier-carved canyon floor. Then it's a smooth 5-hour drive home via CA-99/I-5, closing the loop back in Santa Ana by late afternoon.

Transit Corridor: Kings Canyon (~30 mi from Sequoia) → CA-99 South / I-5 South back to Santa Ana (~5 hrs, ~290 mi).
Loop Closure: Completing the full Sierra Nevada loop back to Santa Ana by 5:30 PM.
8:00 AM

Kings Canyon — Grant Grove & Zumwalt Meadow

General Grant Tree: Walk the short paved loop in Grant Grove to see the "Nation's Christmas Tree" — the second largest living tree on Earth and the only living thing designated a national shrine.

Zumwalt Meadow Boardwalk: Stroll the boardwalk loop along the glacier-carved canyon floor and watch the Kings River reflect raw 8,000-ft granite walls overhead.

12:00 PM

Lunch & Depart for Home

Grab a final lunch in the park or a foothill town, then head home via CA-99 South / I-5 South. The drive takes approximately 5 hours through the Central Valley.

5:30 PM

Loop Complete — Arrive Santa Ana

Return to Orange County with the Sierra Ascent Loop fully closed: ~700 miles, 4 days, 3 nights, and memories of waterfalls, cobalt lakes, and ancient giants.

🏔️ SIERRA ASCENT ROAD TRIP PLAN 🏔️
Departing Santa Ana: Friday July 4th @ 12:00 AM (midnight Thursday night!)
Trip Length: 4 Days & 3 Nights — July 4–7, 2026
Base Camp: Reno, NV Airbnb (Nights 1 & 2)

📍 OUR ROUTE:
Day 1 (Fri Jul 4): Santa Ana → West Shore Tahoe (Emerald Bay + Eagle Falls + Lunch) → Reno Airbnb (Night 1)
Day 2 (Sat Jul 5): Reno → East Shore loop: Sand Harbor, Secret Cove, Skunk Harbor, Cave Rock sunset → back to Reno (Night 2)
Day 3 (Sun Jul 6): Reno → Sequoia NP — Kaweah River swim, Moro Rock, Gen. Sherman Tree (Night 3)
Day 4 (Mon Jul 7): Kings Canyon (Gen. Grant Tree + Zumwalt Meadow) → Home by 5:30 PM

👞 KEY HIKES & SIGHTS:
• Emerald Bay Overlook — 7 AM arrival (+ optional Vikingsholm mansion hike)
• Eagle Falls Trail — ~2 mi round trip from same parking area as Emerald Bay
• Sand Harbor State Park — turquoise water & granite boulders (reserve nevada.com!)
• Secret Cove — hidden Caribbean-colored cove (short trail south on Hwy 28)
• Skunk Harbor — 1.5 mi logging-road hike, 1920s stone picnic house. Pack it out!
• Cave Rock sunset scramble — 180° panorama over the Sierra
• Kaweah River swim + Moro Rock staircase + General Sherman Tree
• General Grant Tree (“Nation’s Christmas Tree”) + Zumwalt Meadow boardwalk

🧳 PACKING ESSENTIALS:
• Warm layers & swim gear
• 6 liters of water capacity per person
• Personal sunscreen and bug spray
• First-aid kit
• Fleece & pants for cold Sequoia evenings after the Sunday swim
• Packed lunch/snacks for Skunk Harbor (no food services on trail)
