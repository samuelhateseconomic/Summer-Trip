Day-by-Day Expedition Itinerary

Friday, July 4th 288 mi Traveled (Stops) + 240 mi to Mammoth

Day 1 — The Dawn Giants & Mountain Crest

Departing Santa Ana at midnight to fully bypass Holiday weekend Southern California freeway bottlenecks. Ascend the Sierra foothills to catch the first light filtering through the world's largest giant sequoia forests.

Transit Corridor: I-5 N over Tejon Pass ➔ CA-99 N ➔ CA-198 E into foothill canyons, then CA-120 (Tioga Pass) or CA-108 over the crest.
Basecamp Target: Alpine hotel lodging in Mammoth Lakes (7,800 ft elevation) for Night 1.
00:00 AM

Midnight Departure

Start the launch from Santa Ana. Enjoy clear open freeway lanes as you drive north through the night.

04:30 AM

Sequoia National Park Arrival

Enter the giant forest gates just as dawn breaks. Catch early light bouncing off the gigantic tree trunks.

Morning Focus

The Giant Sequoias & Moro Rock

General Sherman Tree: Stroll the peaceful walking loop in the quiet dawn hours before the crowds arrive.

Moro Rock: Hike the 350-step granite stairs to catch a stunning 360-degree vista of the Great Western Divide.

Tunnel Log: Drive your vehicle directly through the hollowed-out carcass of a fallen giant tree for a classic group photo.

10:00 AM

Kings Canyon National Park

Transit north on the Generals Highway (30 mi). Visit the **General Grant Tree** (the Nation's Christmas Tree) and stroll the peaceful forest walkways.

12:00 PM

The Great Crest Traverse

Head east over the Sierra mountains using the gorgeous **Tioga Pass (Yosemite CA-120)** or CA-108. Enjoy spectacular granite landscapes rising above the timberline.

05:30 PM

Mammoth Lakes Settle-in

Arrive in Mammoth Lakes. Check-in to your hotel, grab a hearty meal in the alpine village, and relax in nearby geothermal hot springs under the stars.

Scenery & Highlights

Saturday, July 5th 165 mi Traveled

Day 2 — The Cobalt Ascent & Lake Tahoe Relaxation

Depart Mammoth Lakes and travel north along the majestic Eastern Sierra corridor of US-395. Arrive at the dazzling alpine basin of Lake Tahoe to settle in and spend a relaxed day enjoying local city dining, beaches, and watercraft rentals.

Transit Corridor: US-395 North past Mono Lake tufa towers and Sierra passes into South Lake Tahoe.
Basecamp Target: Cozy cabin or resort lodging in South Lake Tahoe for Nights 2 & 3.
09:00 AM

Eastern Sierra Cruise

Depart Mammoth Lakes. Travel along US-395 N. Look out for the otherworldly tufa limestone spires of **Mono Lake** on your right.

12:30 PM

Lake Tahoe Basin Arrival

Arrive in South Lake Tahoe. Capture your first breath-taking glimpse of the cobalt-blue waters. Check in to your lakeside basecamp.

02:00 PM

Lake & City Recreation

Stay local. Rent a kayak, paddleboard, or jet ski in South Lake Tahoe. Float on the crystal clear waters, explore the shoreline, or sunbathe at Pope Beach.

06:00 PM

Cave Rock Sunset Scramble

Drive to Lake Tahoe's East Shore (Nevada side). Do the short, steep scramble up **Cave Rock** to stand atop the giant granite formation, enjoying a panoramic sunset view over the massive lake.

08:00 PM

Lakeside Campfire & Celebration

Head back to South Lake Tahoe for dinner. Gather around a lakeside fire pit, toast marshmallows, and celebrate the journey.

Scenery & Highlights

Sunday, July 6th ~438 mi Round Trip Excursion

Day 3 — The Great Cascade & Eagle Lake Hike

Embark on a scenic highway sprint north to McArthur-Burney Falls Memorial State Park to witness the roaring basalt waterfall. On the way back, head to the beautiful Eagle Lake for a scenic mountain hike.

Transit Corridor: Drive north past Lassen Volcanic National Park to Burney Falls and Eagle Lake, returning to South Lake Tahoe.
Notice: Must reserve a day-use parking pass online in advance due to the holiday weekend.
08:00 AM

Road Trip North

Grab coffees and hit the highway. Enjoy scenic drives passing mountain peaks as you drive north to Burney Falls (approx 4.5 hours drive).

12:30 PM

Burney Falls Basalt Cascade

Marvel at the moss-covered basalt cascade, feel the cold mist on your face, and walk the short loop trail around the waterfall basin. Day-use vehicle fee: $11.

03:30 PM

Eagle Lake Hike

Drive to nearby Eagle Lake. Hike the scenic, rewarding trail to enjoy beautiful mountain views, pine forest scents, and pristine alpine lake waters.

08:30 PM

Reunite & Final Lake Night

Return to South Lake Tahoe for a final celebration dinner. Share pictures from the day, visit local spots, and enjoy the last night of the expedition.

Scenery & Highlights

Monday, July 7th ~450 mi Return Trip

Day 4 — Homeward Bound

Savor a slow, peaceful morning by the lake, pack up your gear, and begin the drive south back to Southern California with memory cards full of mountain views.

Transit Corridor: US-395 South along the Eastern Sierra crest back down to Southern California / Orange County.
08:30 AM

Lakeside Coffee & Breakfast

Enjoy one last hot breakfast in South Lake Tahoe. Walk along the shoreline to watch the morning light reflect off the calm lake surface.

10:30 AM

Load & Check out

Pack up the vehicle cooler, dry bin, and luggage. Make sure no gear is left behind.

11:00 AM

The Southern Descent

Drive south along US-395. Enjoy the scenic drive passing Mt. Whitney and the Alabama Hills, stopping for snacks and fuel along the way.

07:00 PM

Arrive Home

Return to Santa Ana in the evening, concluding the epic Sierra Ascent expedition.

Conclusion of the Journey

Heading back home to Santa Ana. Share your photos and plan your next mountain adventure!

🏔️ SIERRA ASCENT ROAD TRIP 2026 🏔️
Dates: Friday, July 4th – Monday, July 7th
Total Drive: ~693 Miles | ~14 Hours Net Driving

📍 THE ITINERARY:
• Day 1: Santa Ana ➔ Sequoia NP (Sherman Tree & Moro Rock) ➔ Kings Canyon NP (Grant Tree) ➔ Tioga Pass Traverse ➔ Stay Mammoth Lakes
• Day 2: Mammoth Lakes ➔ US-395 Corridor ➔ South Lake Tahoe Arrival ➔ Lake & City Recreation (Kayak/Paddleboard) ➔ Cave Rock Sunset Scramble ➔ Stay South Lake Tahoe
• Day 3: Burney Falls Cascade & Eagle Lake Hike Day Trip (Permit Required!) ➔ Stay South Lake Tahoe
• Day 4: Slow Morning lakeside ➔ Pack up ➔ Drive US-395 South home to Orange County

🎒 KEY ESSENTIALS:
• America the Beautiful National Parks Pass (Samuel Do)
• Burney Falls Permit & Day-Use Pass ($11 - Required)
• Offline GPS Maps downloaded (All - Important!)
• Sunscreen & Mosquito protection (Important!)
• First-Aid Kit (Ready by Samuel Do)
• Prepare $500 cash / emergency funds (All)
• Reusable water container/flasks (min 10L per person)
• Windbreaker/fleece for cold mountain nights (45°F)
• Trail-ready hiking shoes
